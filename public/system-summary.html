1 What Is JORD Golf?

JORD is a web-based tournament management platform for running Longest Drive and Closest to Pin contests at golf events. Players use their phones to scan a QR code on a numbered ball, their GPS distance is calculated automatically, and a live leaderboard updates in real-time for everyone watching.

No app download required. Everything runs in a phone's web browser. Players simply open a link or scan a code.

Core Promise
A player hits their ball, walks up to it, opens a link on their phone, hits submit — and their distance appears on the live leaderboard within seconds.

2 How a Tournament Works — Start to Finish

1
Before the Event — Admin creates the event, draws the hole zones on the satellite map (fairway, rough, out-of-bounds), places the tee boxes and pin, adds ball drop codes to the pool, and prints QR labels to stick on the balls.
↓
2
Players Arrive — A QR code at check-in sends players to the registration page. They enter their ball code + name, pick their tee, name the team. Done in under 2 minutes.
↓
3
On Course — After hitting, players walk to their ball, open the scan link, their phone's GPS locks in. The system auto-detects which zone they're in (fairway, rough, OOB) and pre-selects it. Player hits submit.
↓
4
Live Result — An animated flyover shows a gold line drawing from the tee to the landing spot on a satellite map. The yardage counts up from 0 to final distance. The leaderboard updates in real-time.
↓
5
During the Round — Reps watch the monitor dashboard. If something's wrong, they correct any ball score from the monitor page. Alerts pop up if a player hits "I need help."
↓
6
End of Tournament — Admin clicks "End Tournament." The leaderboard locks, shows the champion card, zone stats, and total yards. Optionally published to the global leaderboard.

3 The Six Pages

Page 1 — Admin Panel /admin

Where you or a tournament director manages everything. Protected by email + password login.

What You Can Do

  • Create and configure events (name, venue, dates, contest types)
  • Draw the hole on a satellite map — trace fairway, rough, and OOB zones by hand. The system auto-clips zones so they never overlap. You can also walk the boundary with your phone and it traces the polygon for you.
  • Place tee boxes (multiple — men's, women's, senior) and the pin
  • Set scoring rules: Does rough count? Does OOB count? What's the penalty?
  • Create a ball pool — bulk add drop codes and print QR labels
  • Watch live registrations and scan submissions
  • Make corrections to any ball score if something goes wrong
  • End the tournament when complete
  • Export all data to CSV

Multi-Admin System

You are the Super Admin. You can create accounts for tournament directors at each event. They only see their own events. You control what each person can do — for example, "allow corrections but not end the tournament."

RoleWhat They Can SeeWhat They Can Do
Super AdminAll events, all adminsEverything, including creating other admins and publishing to global leaderboard
Tournament AdminTheir own events onlyConfigurable per-admin: corrections, end tournament, manage players, manage balls

Page 3 — Scan Page /scan/:code

The page players use on-course after hitting their ball. No login required.

The Player Experience

  • GPS locks in — a badge shows accuracy (waits for a tight reading)
  • The system auto-detects which zone the phone is in and pre-selects it
  • Player confirms the zone (fairway, rough, OOB, or lost ball)
  • Submit → instant animated result

The Result Card Includes

  • Flyover animation — satellite map with a gold line drawing from the tee to the landing spot, then yardage counts up from 0 to final
  • Location badge (fairway, rough, etc.) and yardage
  • Any penalty applied and a plain-English explanation of why
  • A randomized encouragement message based on distance and zone
  • Leaderboard link and admin phone button
  • "I need help" button that sends a GPS-tagged alert to the rep monitor

Page 4 — Live Leaderboard /leaderboard/:eventId

Public page, no login required. This is what goes on the TV at the event.

Features

  • All teams ranked by total yards (LD) or closest to pin (CTP)
  • Click any team to expand and see all 4 players' individual distances
  • Satellite map with colored dots showing where every ball landed — click a team to highlight their dots only
  • Hole Tour button — 29-second animated camera flythrough of the hole (tee → fairway → pin approach → 360° orbit → overhead pullback)
  • TV Mode — full-screen big fonts for display screens and projectors
  • Updates live without refresh using Server-Sent Events

When Tournament Ends

  • Champion card with team name and all 4 players' drives
  • Total yards stat with a fun comparison ("3.2× the Golden Gate Bridge")
  • Zone breakdown grid (fairway / rough / OOB counts)
  • Hall of Fame card — shows if a player set the all-time course record that day

Page 5 — Rep Monitor /monitor

Real-time dashboard for your on-course staff. Password protected.

Features

  • Live satellite map with all player ball positions (colored by team)
  • Real-time team standings with expandable player rows
  • Help alerts — when a player hits "I need help," it pops up here with their GPS coordinates
  • Correction form — click any ball dot on the map to auto-fill the form and override any score
  • Reset scan — clear a player's submission so they can scan again
  • Stats bar — scanned / total / alerts / teams at a glance

Page 6 — Global Leaderboard /global

A public page for bragging rights across all events. No login required.

  • Monthly Top 10 — best fairway drives across all published tournaments that month
  • Course Records — all-time best drive per golf course
  • You (Super Admin) choose which ended events get published here
  • Only fairway drives count — rewards skill, not luck

4 Scoring Logic

Longest Drive

ScenarioHow It Scores
Ball lands in FairwayFull distance. No penalty.
Ball lands in Rough (if rough is allowed)Distance minus perpendicular distance from the fairway edge (you set this rule)
Ball lands OOB (if OOB is allowed)Distance minus a fixed penalty OR minus half the hole length (you choose)
Ball lands in Rough/OOB (if NOT allowed)Zero yards — not scored
Lost ballZero yards — not scored

Closest to Pin

ScenarioHow It Scores
Ball on the greenGPS distance from ball to pin (in feet)
Ball off the greenGPS distance + off-green penalty you set (in feet)
Rangefinder overridePlayer or rep can enter manual feet from a rangefinder — overrides GPS

Team Score

  • LD: Sum of all 4 players' final yards
  • CTP: Best (lowest) distance across all 4 players on the team

5 Database — What Gets Stored

Everything lives in a single SQLite file at data/jord.db. There are 10 tables:

TableWhat It Stores
eventsEvery tournament — name, venue, GPS, zone polygons, scoring rules, status
tee_boxesTee locations per hole (men's, women's, senior)
teamsRegistered teams — name, event, timestamp
ballsEvery ball / drop code — player info, GPS scan data, distances, penalties, final scores
admin_correctionsAudit trail of every manual override made by a rep
rep_alertsPlayer help requests with GPS coordinates
sms_logHistory of all Klaviyo message sends
adminsAdmin accounts — email, hashed password, role, permissions
sessionsActive login tokens (7-day expiry)
password_reset_tokensTemporary reset links (1-hour expiry)

The database also stores 2,700+ US golf courses loaded from a CSV file — used for the venue autocomplete when creating an event. Selecting a course auto-fills the GPS coordinates and flies the map to that location.

6 Pre-Launch Issues to Fix

🚨 Required Before Live Events
HTTPS is mandatory for iPhone GPS. Safari on iPhone blocks geolocation on plain HTTP. Your hosting must have an SSL certificate (HTTPS). Without it, no iPhone user can submit a scan. Free via Let's Encrypt on most hosts.
⚠ Email/SMS Not Connected
Klaviyo is wired into the code but sends are currently mocked — no texts or emails go out. Players don't receive a confirmation after registering. Set client expectations accordingly, or connect Klaviyo before launch.
⚠ Monitor Page Uses Old Auth
The Rep Monitor uses a simple shared password (stored in browser localStorage) rather than the full session-token login system the Admin Panel uses. This means anyone who knows the password can access it. Upgrade before events with paying clients.
Recommended: Add a Backup
The database is a single file. Set up automatic backups (copy the file every 10 minutes during active tournaments) so there's a recovery path if something goes wrong mid-event.

8 What's Technically Strong

  • Real-time, no refresh needed — The leaderboard updates live the moment someone scans, using Server-Sent Events. No polling, no manual refresh.
  • GPS accuracy controls — The scan page shows a live accuracy badge and waits for a tight GPS lock before allowing submission. Players can't submit a wild reading.
  • Zone auto-detection with lock — The phone's GPS is checked against the polygons you drew on the map. The correct zone is pre-selected and locked. Players can't cheat by selecting fairway when they're in the rough.
  • Penalty math is automatic — Perpendicular distance to fairway for rough penalties, half-hole or fixed penalties for OOB. All calculated server-side, never client-side.
  • 48 automated tests — Scoring math and geography logic are tested automatically so future changes don't break things.
  • Concurrent safe — SQLite WAL mode allows multiple players to submit scans at the same time without data corruption.
  • Multi-admin with full audit trail — Every correction is logged with before/after values and a reason. You always know who changed what and why.
  • Course autocomplete from 2,700+ US courses — Setting up a new event auto-fills the GPS coordinates and flies the map to the golf course.
  • No external map tiles needed — ESRI World Imagery satellite tiles are embedded via Mapbox GL JS. No separate satellite tile subscription.

9 Bottom Line

The core product is fully built and works end-to-end. You can run a real tournament with it today. The tech foundation is solid — real-time, GPS-accurate, multi-admin, tested.
